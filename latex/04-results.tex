\subsection{Quantitative Performance}
Four settings are evaluated on the fixed validation split using AP@0.5, AP@0.5:0.95, and Mean IoU.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Validation performance and data composition per training setting. \\
 $N_{\text{real}}$ = number of real training images (unique). \\
 $N_{\text{aug}}$ = number of classical augmentation copies. \\
 $N_{\text{gan}}$ = number of CycleGAN images.}
\begin{tabular}{lrrrrrr}
\toprule
Setting & $N_{\text{real}}$ & $N_{\text{aug}}$ & $N_{\text{gan}}$ & AP@0.5 & AP@0.5:0.95 & Mean IoU \\
\midrule
Baseline & 84 & 0 & 0 & 0.747 & 0.398 & 0.691 \\
Baseline + Classical Aug & 84 & $5\times$84 & 0 & 0.768 & 0.415 & 0.750 \\
Baseline + CycleGAN & 84 & 0 & $1\times$84 & 0.742 & 0.434 & 0.745 \\
Classical Aug + CycleGAN & 84 & $5\times$84 & $1\times$84 & 0.770 & 0.467 & 0.797 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}
\noindent
Overall, settings with classical augmentations improve all metrics over the baseline settings. \\
The baseline + CycleGAN setting alone increases AP@0.5:0.95 and Mean IoU relative to baseline, with a slightly lower AP@0.5. \\
The setting of classical augmentation + CycleGAN has the best performance across all metrics.
